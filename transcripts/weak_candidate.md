## Rewritten Answer Example

**Original:**
"Um, so like, I've worked on some pretty complex things I think. We built a, um, like a dashboard..."

**Improved:**
"I led the backend development for an internal analytics dashboard that aggregated data from three microservices into a single view used by our operations team. The most complex decision I made was how to handle data freshness — we needed near-realtime updates but couldn't afford to poll every second at scale. I implemented a WebSocket layer using Socket.IO that pushed updates only when the underlying data changed, which reduced unnecessary server load by about 60% compared to our initial polling approach."

**What changed:**
- Removed 12 filler words/phrases ("um", "like", "sort of", "I think", "basically")
- Replaced "I sort of helped" with "I led" — establishes clear ownership
- Added STAR structure: Situation (analytics dashboard), Task (backend development), Action (WebSocket implementation), Result (60% load reduction)
- Replaced vague "it was complex" with a specific technical problem and solution

## Final Coaching Feedback (excerpt)

**Average Score:** 2.3/10

## Weaknesses
The candidate consistently deflected ownership ("someone else decided", "I mostly did frontend") and provided answers with no concrete examples, metrics, or technical depth...

## What To Practice
1. STAR method — every behavioral answer must have a Situation, Task, specific Action YOU took, and a Result with a number
2. Remove filler words — record yourself answering and listen back
3. Prepare 5 ownership stories where YOU made a decision and it had measurable impact
